User's Guide to the Slider widget

The Slider widget displays a movable "bar" that slides along a vertical "trough." It performs the same function as the Tk scale widget but looks more like a physical slider, so works better in "control panels" and similar styles of interface.

At the top of the slider is a display of the current value. Below that is the trough and slider bar, and at the bottom a label. To move the slider and change its value, simply click on the bar with the left button and drag it. If the resolution of the slider has been set to a non-zero value, the slider jumps in appropriate increments; if not, it moves smoothly.

There are two other ways of moving the slider: clicking with the left button anywhere near the slider will cause the slider to repeatedly move one step (one resolution increment if non-zero, else one pixel) at a time. There is a delay between the first and second steps of about 400 ms; after, steps repeat at about 10 times per second. The bar steps moving when it reaches the mouse cursor.

Clicking with the middle button anywhere near the trough makes the bar "jump" to the mouse position. If the button remains held down while the mouse is moved, the bar follows the mouse.
